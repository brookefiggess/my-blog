What went well?

This week was productive for me. I successfully presented the first iteration of my hi-fi prototype. Receiving feedback throughout the week, (especially during my WIP presentation) helped me clarify the direction of my design and make necessary refinements. I also made steady progress without any blockers, which allowed me to maintain momentum across the week. Another highlight was getting a better understanding of how the database could integrate with my project, giving me more clarity on the technical side.

What didn't go well?

Early in the week, I underestimated the presentation component of my hi-fi, which meant I had to play catch-up to ensure everything was clearly communicated. While I began looking into how to implement a database, I didn’t dive as deep as I’d hoped on the visual side of things, so that area still feels a little uncertain. There’s also room to improve how I translate feedback into quick design iterations while balancing the technical requirements.

What can be improved?

Allocating set time blocks to technical development (not just design) will help ensure both sides of the project evolve together. I also want to become more intentional with how I document feedback and translate it into my iterations more efficiently.

What actions can I take for the next sprint?

Next sprint, I’ll prioritise clarifying the database requirements and begin actively implementing a basic structure for it. I’ll also work on refining my second hi-fi prototype based on the latest feedback. To prepare for the final presentation, I’ll allocate time earlier in the week for scripting and slide prep. Lastly, I’ll plan to conduct a quick round of user testing to validate whether the updated design changes are effective.
